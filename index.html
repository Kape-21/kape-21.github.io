Advanced Procedures & Techniques

Dr. Jay Deep Ghosh is known for using several advanced and minimally invasive techniques in both spine and joint surgery, with a strong focus on reducing pain, blood loss, and recovery time for patients.

Advanced Spine Procedures

Uses small incisions, tubular retractors, and specialised instruments to treat conditions like **slipped disc, spinal stenosis, and spondylolisthesis** while preserving muscles and soft tissue.

Benefits typically include less blood loss, shorter hospital stay, faster mobilisation, and smaller scars compared with conventional open surgery.

More Details on MISS

Involves inserting a **high‑definition endoscope through a keyhole incision** to visualise and remove herniated disc fragments or decompress pinched nerves, often under local or epidural anaesthesia.

This technique allows targeted decompression with minimal tissue disruption and is particularly highlighted by Dr. Ghosh in his social media and hospital outreach as “latest minimally invasive spine surgery” for disc prolapse and canal stenosis.

More Details on Endoscopy

For lumbar canal stenosis and some traumatic or degenerative instabilities, he uses **MISS approaches with percutaneous screws and rods** to decompress the spinal canal and stabilise segments without large midline exposure.

These procedures aim to maintain spinal alignment and stability while decreasing postoperative pain and rehabilitation time compared with standard open fusion surgeries.

More Details on Stabilisation

Advanced Joint and Orthopaedic Procedures

Uses a **fibre‑optic camera (arthroscope) and micro‑instruments** through tiny portals to treat knee, shoulder, and other joint problems such as meniscal tears, ACL injuries, and shoulder impingement.

Arthroscopy is emphasised as one of his special interests and is valued for quicker recovery, less postoperative stiffness, and early return to activities.

More Details on Arthroscopy

Performs contemporary TKR techniques that use **pre‑operative planning, refined soft‑tissue balancing, and high‑performance implants** to restore alignment and function in severe arthritis.

These replacements are designed for long-term durability and enhanced range of motion, with multimodal pain control and early physiotherapy to fast-track recovery.

More Details on TKR

Utilises **advanced bearing surfaces** and cemented or uncemented implants to relieve pain from hip arthritis, avascular necrosis, or fracture‑related damage.

Modern THR techniques aim to preserve muscle, improve stability, and allow patients to walk with support within a day or two after surgery in most routine cases.
